The Four Factors of winning basketball

Developed by Dean Oliver, the Four Factors identify the statistical drivers of wins — listed in order of impact: Shooting Efficiency (40%), Turnover Rate (25%), Offensive Rebounding (20%), and Free Throw Rate (15%). Improving in any factor produces real wins. Improving in all four produces a championship contender.

How to read advanced stats

Player Archetypes
Each player is assigned an archetype based on their statistical profile — reflecting the role they actually fill on the court. Archetypes determine how skill areas are weighted in the evaluation engine, ensuring players are judged by the standards of their role.
Roster Archetype Distribution
System Methodology

The archetype system classifies players using a rules-based scoring approach applied to their statistical profile. Key indicators include usage rate, assist rate, three-point attempt rate, rebounding rate, and defensive activity.

Each archetype carries a unique weight profile across the five skill areas. A Primary Creator, for example, is weighted more heavily on Playmaking and Shooting, while a 3-and-D Forward is evaluated more on Defense.

This ensures that every player is compared to relevant peers — guards with similar usage and position, forwards with comparable role responsibilities — rather than a single conference-wide benchmark.
